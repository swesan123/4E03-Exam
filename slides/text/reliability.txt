--- PAGE 16 ---
IFR and DFR
• IFR (Increasing Failure Rate) - ℎ 7 is a nondecreasing
function of 7
• IFR – more likely to fail as they age
• DFR (Decreasing Failure Rate) - ℎ(7) is nonincreasing
function of 7
• DFR – less likely to fail as they age
• In general, distributions may be neither IFR nor DFR
• so-called “bathtub curve”
• range over which failure rate is constant is quite often the
useful life

--- PAGE 17 ---
IFR and DFR - Implications
• DFR – never preemptively replace a component
• IFR – may become desirable to preemptively replace a
component before it fails

--- PAGE 20 ---
Machine Repair Problem
• F machines, each fail independently with rate B
• G servers for repairs, each work on a single machine with
repair rate H
• This is a birth-death process, proceed as with CTMC material
