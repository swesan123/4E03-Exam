Random Variables
• A random variable X (capital letters) is a real-valued function

• Examples:
1. The sum of the rolls of two dice

--- PAGE 35 ---
Memoryless Property
• This is known as the “memoryless” property, the future is
independent of the past, given the present. Knowing that
time 𝑏 has elapsed gives no information about the future.
• Fact: the exponential distribution is the only continuous
distribution with the memoryless property.
• Often used to model phenomena with this property: if we
assume memoryless, then the underlying distribution is
exponential.
